Global Arms Trades (1950-2023)

by Calvin Lu


Interactive data visualization of international arms sales.


This project presents a visualization of international arms deals spanning from 1950 to 2023, offering an immersive exploration into the global dynamics of military trade. International arms deals play a influence geopolitical landscapes, acting as a catalyst and a consequence of international conflicts. By providing or withholding military support, countries wield significant influence over regional and global stability. The influence of politics on arms trades may be stronger during periods of conflict, where the category and volume of arms supplied may impact the duration, intensity, and outcome of wars.


I hypothesize that international arms deals will be more frequent to countries currently in conflict.


The data driving the visualizations are sourced from the Stockholm International Peace Research Institute SIPRI Arms Transfer database annual military arms transfers and the Correlates of War interstate conflict database.
