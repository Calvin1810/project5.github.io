Population Change in the United States (1910-2020)

by Calvin Lu


Trivariate data visualization on population across the United States.


The project will illustrate changes in population of states across the United States from 1910 to 2020. Population change influences various socio-economic factors and public policies. Since 1910, the U.S. has undergone significant shifts in population distribution due to factors such as urbanization and economic expansion. By illustrating population changes over time, the visualizations will display patterns in population fluctuations for states across the U.S. This project will generate a deeper understanding of population shifts and spark new inquiries for implications concerning economic prosperity and political representation.


I hypothesize that the highest population change will shift from the West during the first half of the 20th century to the South until the present.


We will be analyzing the evolution of population change across the United States. The data driving this graph is sourced from the United States Census Bureau data centers on Resident Population Tables. The maps to the right utilizes a slider to display the state percentage of national population and percent change in state population during that decade between 1910-2020.
